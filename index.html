About

Researcher focused on resilient habitat operations

I'm Luca Vaccino, a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering. My work spans modeling, simulation, and scheduling for complex space systems, with a focus on resilient operations and transition planning for habitats.

Recent efforts include research on autonomous transition scheduling, experimental validation on a cyber-physical testbed, and contributions to student/NASA programs.

Master’s Thesis — Condition Monitoring of Hydraulic Pumps

Politecnico di Torino (2021). Built a lumped-parameter digital twin of an axial piston pump and trained SVM, k-NN, and Decision Tree models to detect component faults. Validated on healthy and “extremely damaged” valve-plate tests with <5% drain-flow error at 1500–2000 rpm.
